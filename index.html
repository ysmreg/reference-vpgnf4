Buying Your First Rolex: The 2026 Datejust or the Oyster Perpetual?

Every first-time Rolex buyer arrives at the same fork in the road: Datejust or Oyster Perpetual. Both are time-only watches in the Professional collection. Both are available in 36mm and 41mm. Both share the same calibre 3230 movement. The differences are smaller than the internet suggests, but they matter.

The case for the Datejust

The Datejust adds a date window at three o'clock with the signature Cyclops magnification lens. It offers a wider range of dial colors, bezel options (smooth, fluted, diamond-set), and bracelet choices (Oyster or Jubilee). The variety means you can configure a Datejust that ranges from understated to flashy depending on the combination. Retail starts at approximately CHF 8,100 for the steel 36mm on Oyster bracelet.

The case for the Oyster Perpetual

The OP strips everything down to essentials. No date, no Cyclops, smooth bezel only, Oyster bracelet only. The result is the cleanest dial in the Rolex catalog. The OP is also roughly CHF 2,000 less expensive than the equivalent Datejust, making it the most accessible new Rolex. The 2026 color expansion — including the viral turquoise and candy pink dials — has turned the OP from a starter watch into a collector target.

Which holds value better

Historically, the Datejust holds value well but does not typically appreciate above retail unless it is a special configuration. The OP has been the surprise performer in recent years — certain dial colors trade at significant premiums. If pure financial return matters, the OP with a desirable dial color has the edge. If daily utility matters, the Datejust's date function wins.

The honest answer

Buy whichever one you cannot stop thinking about. Both are exceptional watches that will last generations. A first Rolex is not an investment thesis — it is a milestone purchase. Let your gut make the call.
